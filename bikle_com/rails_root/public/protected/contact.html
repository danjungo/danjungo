Are you receiving spam with a return address of someone@bikle.com?

If so, It's forged e-mail.

The domain bikle.com is configured to prevent ANY e-mail from being sent.

If Dan Bikle needs to send e-mail, he uses gmail with
bikle@bikle.com
as a return address.

If you send e-mail to bikle@bikle.com, it actually gets routed to Google via
an internet mechanism known as an MX record.

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MX_record

If you have a domain and you want to use Google to receive e-mail for you,
that is an excellent choice.

Why?

Google offers an extremely effective self-learning spam filter.

By itself, it does a good job of filtering spam.

After a few weeks of watching you (and millions of others)
tag the few spams which do get through, it becomes extremely accurate.


The user interface to Google hosted e-mail is identical to gmail.com.

You can find out more info here:
http://www.google.com/apps/intl/en/group/index.html
